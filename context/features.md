# Workflow 1: Creator → Brand

**Problem:** Creators have an audience but no structured way to prove its value, no visibility into which brands are actively spending, and no leverage in negotiation because they lack data about themselves.

**Solution:** Give creators a dashboard they've never had. YouTube Studio shows analytics. Vettd shows how brands *see* you.

---

## 1. Discovery & Positioning

### Creator Profile Card — the "media kit killer"
A live shareable URL (`vettd.com/c/mkbhd`) that replaces the static PDF media kit. Any brand that visits sees real-time Vettd Score, engagement rate, performance trend, audience interest graph, and authenticity score. Always current — the data sells, not the creator.

Includes an **automated outreach generator** that drafts brand-specific pitch messages using live metrics and audience data. The creator fills in the target brand; Claude writes the pitch with real numbers pulled from their profile.

### Niche Positioning Score
Shows where the creator ranks within their cluster. *"You're in the top 12% of tech creators in your subscriber tier for engagement rate."* That's a negotiation card — concrete proof of relative value.

### Audience Interest Expansion
Most creators think of themselves narrowly. A gaming creator might not realize their audience over-indexes on energy drinks, mechanical keyboards, and Japan travel. Surface the lateral interests of their audience — those are the real brand opportunities. *"Brands in these adjacent categories have high match scores with your audience."*

### Performance Trend Narrative
Auto-generated 90-day summary in plain English. *"Your average views per video increased 34% over the past 3 months. Strongest format: product reviews. Optimal length: under 12 minutes."* This becomes the opening paragraph of every brand pitch.

---

## 2. Brand Discovery

### Brand Opportunity Feed
Active brands ranked by audience match score against the creator's profile. Not a generic directory — personalised and filtered by the creator's category preferences (open to tech, gaming, food — not alcohol, gambling).

### Cold Outreach Generator
Creator selects a target brand. Claude drafts a pitch using actual metrics: *"Hi [Brand], my last 20 videos averaged 180K views with a 4.2% engagement rate. My audience's top interests beyond [my niche] include [X, Y, Z], which aligns with your [product] targeting [demographic]."* Every number pulled live.

### Deal History & Rate Card
Creators log past brand deals (brand, rate, format, performance). Vettd uses this to benchmark their market rate. *"Creators with your Vettd Score in your tier typically command $800–$2,400 per integration."* Prevents systematic undercharging.

### Brand Fit Preview
Before pitching, the creator sees a simulated match score. *"If [Brand] searched right now, you'd rank #3 for their target profile."* Shows what to improve to move up. Creates a feedback loop.

---

## 3. Reputation & Trust

### Verified Metrics Badge
Embeddable "Vettd Verified" badge for external profiles, websites, and email signatures. Links back to the live profile. Proof that numbers are not self-reported.

### Past Campaign Performance
After a brand deal, both sides log outcomes — impressions delivered, click-through if tracked, overall satisfaction. Over time creators build a verified track record. *"3 completed campaigns, 100% delivery, avg 12% above projected views."*

# Workflow 2: Brand → Creator

**Problem:** Brands either go through an agency (expensive, biased) or do manual research (slow, gut-feel). They have a brief but no structured way to express it, and no objective basis for comparing creator options.

---

## 1. Discovery

### Brief-First Search
The brand describes their campaign — product category, target audience, goal, budget. Claude converts this into a scored creator shortlist. The brief *is* the search query. No dropdowns required.

### Audience Overlap Calculator
Brand inputs their existing customer profile (age, gender, interests, location). Vettd scores every creator by how closely their inferred audience matches. Not *"this creator makes fitness content"* but *"this creator's audience is 68% male, 25–34, clustering around performance nutrition and gym culture — your exact buyer."*

### Whitespace Discovery
Shows adjacent clusters with matching audience demographics but zero competitor sponsorship. *"Your competitors are concentrated in fitness. These travel and lifestyle creators have similar demographics but no competing deals."* Helps brands find underpriced inventory.

### Budget Optimizer
Brand inputs total budget. Vettd models two strategies: one mega creator vs. a cluster of 4–6 mid-tier creators with comparable total reach but higher combined engagement and better cost-per-view. Usually the cluster wins. Shows the case with actual numbers.

---

## 2. Evaluation

### Side-by-Side Comparison with Campaign Projections
Not just metrics — projected outcomes for the brand's specific brief. *"For your $5K budget targeting 18–34 fitness enthusiasts: Creator A delivers 240K reach at $0.021 CPE. Creator B delivers 180K reach at $0.018 CPE but higher conversion potential given audience intent signals."*

### Fake Engagement Red Flag Report
One-click audit before committing budget. View spike history, bot likelihood score, comment quality assessment, ghost follower estimate. Presented as a traffic light: green / amber / red.

### Historical Consistency Report
Variance analysis across the last 50 videos. A creator with steady 80K views is more reliable than one averaging 300K driven by two outliers. This surfaces the difference.

### Comment Sentiment Analysis
Claude categorises sampled comments across recent videos: purchase intent language, geographic cues, age/life-stage indicators, audience identity signals. *"23% of sampled comments contain travel planning intent. 18% reference disposable income. 11% mention Japan specifically."* Audience intelligence no media kit provides.

---

## 3. Campaign Management

### Shortlist & Brief Sharing
Brand curates 3–5 creators. Vettd generates a shareable report — creator profiles, match scores, projected metrics, cost estimates. Presentation-ready for internal sign-off. No spreadsheet assembly.

### Outreach Templates
Once a creator is shortlisted, Vettd drafts personalised outreach referencing specific recent content. Not a generic blast.

### Campaign Tracker
After a deal is live, brand logs the video URL. Vettd pulls daily stats and compares against projected reach. *"Video is at 78% of projected views at day 7 — on track."* Creates accountability and a feedback loop.

### ROI Attribution Model
Brand inputs post-campaign data — promo code usage, affiliate clicks, UTM traffic. Vettd calculates actual CPE and cost per conversion vs. projection. Over time, builds a brand-specific performance model. *"Your best-performing creator type historically: mid-tier gaming, 200K–500K subs, 3–4% ER."*
