# **Workflow 1: Creator → Brand**

**Problem:** Creators have an audience but no structured way to prove its value, no visibility into which brands are actively spending, and no leverage in negotiation because they don't have data about themselves.

**Solution:** Give creators a dashboard about their own channel that they've never had. YouTube Studio shows you your analytics. Vettd shows you how brands *see* you.

Any brand that visits it sees real-time Vettd Score, engagement rate, recent performance trend, audience interest graph, authenticity score. The creator doesn't have to sell — the data does.

   2) ### **Niche Positioning Score**:Tell the creator where they sit within their cluster. "You're in the top 12% of tech creators in your subscriber tier for engagement rate." That's a negotiation card. They can show a brand exactly why they're worth more than the next creator in the list.

   3) ### **Audience Interest Expansion** Most creators think of themselves narrowly. A gaming creator might not realize their audience also over-indexes on energy drinks, mechanical keyboards, and Japan travel. Show them the lateral interests of their audience — those are their real brand opportunities. "Brands in these adjacent categories have high match scores with your audience."

   4) ### **Performance Trend Narrative** Auto-generate a plain-English summary of their last 90 days. "Your average views per video increased 34% over the past 3 months. Your most engaging content is product reviews. Your audience responds most to videos under 12 minutes." This becomes the opening paragraph of every brand pitch.

2) **Brand Discovery**  
   1) **Brand Opportunity Feed** Brands that register on Vettd (or that creators manually add) get surfaced to matching creators. "These 8 brands are actively looking for creators in your niche with your audience profile." Creators opt into visibility for specific brand categories — they can say "I'm open to tech, gaming, food. Not alcohol, gambling."  
   2) **Cold Outreach Generator** Creator fills in which brand they want to work with. Claude drafts a pitch using their actual metrics. "Hi \[Brand\], I'm reaching out because my last 20 videos averaged 180K views with a 4.2% engagement rate. My audience's top interests beyond \[my niche\] include \[X, Y, Z\], which aligns with your \[product\] targeting \[demographic\]." Every number is pulled live from their profile.  
   3) **Deal History & Rate Card** Creators can log past brand deals (brand name, rate, format, performance). Vettd uses this to suggest their market rate relative to similar creators. "Creators with your Vettd Score in your tier typically command $800–$2,400 per integration." Helps them stop undercharging.  
   4) **Brand Fit Preview** Before a creator pitches a brand, show them a simulated match score. "If \[Brand\] searched for creators right now, you'd rank \#3 for their target profile." Shows them what to improve if they want to move up. Creates a feedback loop.

3) ### **Reputation & Trust**

   1) ### **Verified Metrics Badge** Creators can display a "Vettd Verified" badge on their external profiles, websites, emails. It links back to their live profile. Proof that their numbers aren't self-reported.

   2) ### **Past Campaign Performance** After a brand deal, both sides can log outcomes — impressions delivered, click-through if tracked, overall satisfaction. Over time creators build a track record. "3 completed campaigns, 100% delivery, avg 12% above projected views."

## **Workflow 2: Brand → Creator**

**The brand's problem:** They're either going through an agency (expensive, biased) or doing manual research (slow, gut-feel). They have a brief but no structured way to express it, and no objective basis for comparing creator options.

---

1. ### **Discovery**

   1. ### **Brief-First Search** Brand doesn't search for creators — they describe their campaign. Product category, target audience, goal, budget. Claude converts this into a scored creator shortlist. The brief is the search query

   2. ### **Audience Overlap Calculator** Brand has an existing customer profile — age range, interests, location, gender skew. They input it. Vettd scores every creator by how closely their inferred audience matches. Not just "this creator makes fitness content" but "this creator's audience is 68% male, 25–34, interest clusters around performance nutrition and gym culture — your product's exact buyer."

   3. ### **Whitespace Discovery** Brand searches for creators in their category. Vettd also shows clusters that are *adjacent* but less saturated. "Your competitors are concentrated in fitness. These travel and lifestyle creators have similar audience demographics but zero competitor sponsorship." Helps brands find underpriced inventory.

   4. ### **Budget Optimizer** Brand inputs total budget. Vettd shows two options: one mega creator, or a cluster of 4–6 mid-tier creators with comparable total reach but higher combined engagement and better cost-per-view. Usually the cluster wins. Makes the case with actual numbers.

2. ### **Evaluation**

   1. ### **Side-by-Side Comparison with Campaign Projections** Not just metrics comparison — projected outcomes for *their specific brief*. "For your $5,000 budget targeting 18–34 fitness enthusiasts: Creator A delivers 240K estimated reach at $0.021 CPE. Creator B delivers 180K reach at $0.018 CPE but higher conversion potential given audience intent signals."

   2. ### **Fake Engagement Red Flag Report** One-click audit. Before committing budget, brand gets a risk summary. View spike history, bot likelihood score, comment quality assessment, ghost follower estimate. Presented as a simple traffic light. Green/amber/red.

   3. ### **Historical Consistency Report** Not just recent performance — how consistent has this creator been over 6 months? Do their numbers hold up or are they riding one viral video? Variance analysis on the last 50 videos. A creator with steady 80K views is often more valuable than one with 300K average driven by two outliers.

   4. ### **Comment Sentiment Analysis** Pull top comments across last 10 videos. Claude categorizes them: purchase intent language, audience identity signals, geographic cues, age/life-stage hints. Brand sees: "23% of sampled comments contain travel planning intent. 18% reference having disposable income. 11% mention Japan specifically." That's audience intelligence that no media kit provides.

3. ### **Campaign Management**

   1. ### **Shortlist & Brief Sharing** Brand curates a shortlist of 3–5 creators. Vettd generates a shareable report PDF — creator profiles, match scores, projected metrics, cost estimates. Internal presentation-ready. No spreadsheet assembly required.

   2. ### **Outreach Templates** Once a creator is shortlisted, Vettd drafts the outreach email. Personalised to the creator's content style and recent videos. References specific content. Not a generic blast.

   3. ### **Campaign Tracker** After a deal is live, brand logs the live video. Vettd pulls the video's stats daily and compares against the projected reach. "Video is at 78% of projected views at day 7 — on track." Creates accountability and a feedback loop for future campaigns.

   4. ### **ROI Attribution Model** Brand inputs post-campaign data — promo code usage, affiliate link clicks, UTM traffic. Vettd calculates actual CPE, cost per conversion, compares to projection. Over time, builds a brand-specific performance model. "Your best-performing creator type historically: mid-tier gaming, 200K–500K subs, 3–4% ER."
